Bankruptcy Filing Checklist for New York

Everything you need to gather and complete before filing bankruptcy in the Southern District of New York. Print this page and check off items as you go.

Required Documents

Income Documentation

  • Pay stubs - last 6 months (all jobs)
  • Federal tax returns - last 2 years (with all schedules)
  • New York State tax returns - last 2 years
  • NYC tax returns - last 2 years (if applicable)
  • Social Security or pension statements - if receiving benefits
  • Self-employment records - profit/loss statements, 1099s
  • Other income documentation - alimony, rental income, side jobs

Bank and Financial Accounts

  • Bank statements - last 6 months (all accounts, including closed ones)
  • Investment account statements - stocks, bonds, mutual funds
  • Retirement account statements - 401(k), IRA, pension (most recent)
  • Life insurance policies - with cash value information

Debt Documentation

  • Credit report - pull free copies from all 3 bureaus (AnnualCreditReport.com)
  • List of all creditors - with account numbers, balances, and addresses
  • Collection letters and notices
  • Lawsuit papers - any pending or recent lawsuits
  • Judgment records - any money judgments against you
  • Garnishment orders - current or recent

Property and Asset Documentation

  • Mortgage statements - current balance, monthly payment
  • Property deed or co-op proprietary lease
  • Property tax assessment - current market value
  • Vehicle titles and registration
  • Vehicle loan/lease agreements
  • Estimated values of vehicles (use KBB or NADA)
  • Valuable personal property - jewelry, electronics, collections

Other Documents

  • Government-issued photo ID - for the 341 meeting
  • Social Security card or proof of SSN
  • Domestic support orders - child support, alimony
  • Divorce decree or separation agreement (if applicable)
  • Insurance policies - homeowner's, auto, health
  • Lease or rental agreement

Required Steps Before Filing

  • Complete credit counseling course - Must be from a DOJ-approved provider. Costs $15-25. Can be done online in about 1 hour. Must be completed within 180 days before filing. Keep the certificate.
  • Choose state or federal exemptions - New York allows you to pick one system. State exemptions are better for homeowners (homestead $179,975-$215,550). Federal may be better for renters (larger wildcard).
  • Determine which chapter to file - Chapter 7 (debt elimination, 3-4 months) or Chapter 13 (repayment plan, 3-5 years). The means test determines Chapter 7 eligibility.
  • Pull your credit reports - Verify all debts are accounted for. Dispute errors. Free at AnnualCreditReport.com.
  • Stop certain activities - No new credit card charges, no property transfers, no repaying family loans, no cash advances.

After Filing: Remaining Steps

  • Attend the 341 meeting - Usually 21-40 days after filing. Bring photo ID and SSN proof.
  • Complete debtor education course - Second required course ($10-25). Must be completed before discharge. File the certificate with the court.
  • Provide tax return to trustee - The trustee may request your most recent tax return (must be provided within 7 days of request).
  • Respond to trustee requests - Provide any additional documents the trustee requests promptly.
  • Make plan payments (Chapter 13 only) - First payment due within 30 days of filing.

Things to Avoid Before Filing

  • Do not run up credit card debt. Purchases over $800 for luxury goods within 90 days may be challenged as non-dischargeable.
  • Do not take cash advances. Cash advances over $1,100 within 70 days are presumptively non-dischargeable.
  • Do not transfer property. Moving assets to friends, family, or trusts can be reversed by the trustee and may constitute fraud.
  • Do not repay family loans. Paying back a relative within a year before filing is a preferential transfer. The trustee can recover the money from your relative.
  • Do not empty retirement accounts. Retirement funds are fully exempt in bankruptcy - using them to pay debts first is unnecessary.
  • Do not hide assets. Concealing assets is a federal crime that can result in denial of discharge and criminal prosecution.

Frequently Asked Questions

What documents do I need to file bankruptcy?

Tax returns (2 years), pay stubs (6 months), bank statements (6 months), list of all debts and assets, vehicle titles, mortgage statements, and your credit counseling certificate. Full guide.

Do I need to take a credit counseling course?

Yes, two courses are required: credit counseling before filing ($15-25, about 1 hour online) and debtor education after filing but before discharge ($10-25). Both must be from DOJ-approved providers.

How long does it take to prepare?

Usually 1-4 weeks. Emergency filings can be done in a few days if facing imminent garnishment, repossession, or foreclosure. See full timeline.

What should I stop doing before filing?

Avoid running up new debt, transferring property, repaying family loans, taking cash advances, or selling assets below market value. These can create problems in your case.
