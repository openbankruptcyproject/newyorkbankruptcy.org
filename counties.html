Bankruptcy by County -- New York Metro Area

Your county determines which federal court handles your bankruptcy case and which state exemptions protect your property. Find your county below for specific information.

5 Counties in the New York Metro

New York County (Manhattan)

New York County (Manhattan) is the financial capital of the world and the most densely populated county in the United States. Bankruptcy cases are handled by the Southern District of New York, one of the busiest bankruptcy courts in the nation.

District: Southern District of New York (Manhattan Division)
Courthouse: One Bowling Green, New York, NY 10004
New York County (Manhattan) bankruptcy guide →

Kings County (Brooklyn)

Kings County (Brooklyn) is the most populous borough in New York City. Unlike Manhattan, Brooklyn falls under the Eastern District of New York -- a different federal court district with its own courthouse and judges.

District: Eastern District of New York (Brooklyn Division)
Courthouse: 271-C Cadman Plaza East, Brooklyn, NY 11201
Kings County (Brooklyn) bankruptcy guide →

Queens County

Queens County is the most ethnically diverse urban area in the world and the second-largest New York City borough by population. Cases are filed in the Eastern District of New York.

District: Eastern District of New York (Brooklyn Division)
Courthouse: 271-C Cadman Plaza East, Brooklyn, NY 11201
Queens County bankruptcy guide →

Bronx County

Bronx County is the northernmost borough of New York City. Like Manhattan, it falls under the Southern District of New York. Bronx residents file at the Manhattan courthouse.

District: Southern District of New York (Manhattan Division)
Courthouse: One Bowling Green, New York, NY 10004
Bronx County bankruptcy guide →

Westchester County

Westchester County is directly north of New York City and includes affluent suburbs like Scarsdale, White Plains, and New Rochelle. It has its own division within the Southern District of New York.

District: Southern District of New York (White Plains Division)
Courthouse: 300 Quarropas St, White Plains, NY 10601

Frequently Asked Questions

Which counties are in the New York bankruptcy metro area?

The New York metro area covers 5 counties: New York County (Manhattan), Kings County (Brooklyn), Queens County, Bronx County, Westchester County. Each county falls under a specific federal bankruptcy court district.

Does it matter which county I live in for bankruptcy?

Yes. Your county determines which federal district court handles your case, and potentially which state exemptions apply. Different districts may have different local rules, trustees, and dismissal rates.
