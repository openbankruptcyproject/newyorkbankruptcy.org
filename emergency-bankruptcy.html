Emergency Bankruptcy Filing in New York

When a foreclosure sale, wage garnishment, or vehicle repossession is hours or days away, an emergency bankruptcy filing can stop it immediately. Here is how it works in the Southern District of New York.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

How Emergency Filing Works

An emergency (or "bare-bones") bankruptcy filing lets you activate the automatic stay immediately by filing just a minimal petition. You then have 14 days to complete the rest of your paperwork.

1

File the Petition

File Official Form 101, a creditor matrix (list of creditors with addresses), and your credit counseling certificate. This can be done electronically through the S.D.N.Y. system.

2

Stay Activates

The automatic stay takes effect the instant the petition is filed. Creditors must stop all collection, foreclosure, and garnishment activity.

3

Complete in 14 Days

File your full schedules, statement of financial affairs, means test, and all remaining documents within 14 days or the case may be dismissed.

When You Might Need an Emergency Filing

  • Foreclosure sale is imminent. New York uses judicial foreclosure (12-18 months typical), but once a sale date is set, you may need to act fast. Filing before the sale stops it cold.
  • Wage garnishment just started. New York allows garnishment of 10% of gross or 25% of disposable wages (whichever is less). Filing stops the garnishment immediately.
  • Vehicle repossession is threatened. If a repo agent is coming, filing activates the stay and makes repossession a violation of federal law.
  • Bank account levy. A creditor with a judgment can freeze your bank account in New York. An emergency filing stops this.
  • Utility shutoff. Filing gives you 20 days of protection from utility disconnection.

What You Need to File

Day 1 (Emergency)

  • Voluntary Petition (Official Form 101)
  • Creditor matrix (names and addresses)
  • Credit counseling certificate
  • Filing fee ($338 for Chapter 7, $313 for Chapter 13) or fee waiver application
  • Social Security number statement

Within 14 Days

  • Schedules A/B through J (all assets, debts, income, expenses)
  • Statement of Financial Affairs
  • Means Test (Chapter 7) or Disposable Income (Chapter 13)
  • Tax returns (last 4 years if requested by trustee)
  • Pay stubs for the last 60 days
  • Chapter 13 plan (if filing Chapter 13)

New York-Specific Considerations

S.D.N.Y. Courthouse Locations

The Southern District of New York has courthouses at One Bowling Green, Manhattan and 300 Quarropas Street, White Plains. Electronic filing is available 24/7 through the court's CM/ECF system.

New York Exemptions Still Apply

Even in an emergency filing, your New York exemptions protect your property. New York allows you to choose between state and federal exemptions. The state homestead exemption ranges from $179,975 to $215,550 depending on your county, and the vehicle exemption is $4,825.

Repeat Filer Warning

If you had a bankruptcy case dismissed within the past year, the automatic stay from an emergency filing only lasts 30 days (11 U.S.C. section 362(c)(3)). Two or more prior dismissals means no stay at all without a court order. Check your eligibility with the 1328(f) screener.

Risks of Emergency Filing

  • Dismissal risk. If you miss the 14-day deadline for remaining documents, your case is dismissed and creditors can resume collection.
  • Incomplete strategy. Rushing to file means less time to choose the right chapter (7 vs. 13) and plan your exemptions.
  • Refiling restrictions. A dismissed case can trigger the 30-day stay limitation if you need to file again within a year.
  • Creditor matrix errors. Leaving creditors off the emergency matrix means they will not receive notice of the stay.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is an emergency bankruptcy filing?

An emergency bankruptcy filing (also called a bare-bones or skeleton petition) is when you file just the petition and a few required documents to get an immediate case number and activate the automatic stay. You then have 14 days to file your remaining schedules and documents. This is used when you need to stop a foreclosure sale, repossession, or wage garnishment immediately.

How fast can I file emergency bankruptcy in New York?

An emergency bankruptcy can be filed the same day you decide to proceed. The S.D.N.Y. courthouse accepts filings electronically, and the automatic stay takes effect the moment the petition is filed. If a foreclosure sale is scheduled for tomorrow, filing today will stop it.

What documents do I need for an emergency filing?

At minimum you need the petition (Official Form 101), a list of creditors with addresses (the creditor matrix), and your credit counseling certificate. You have 14 days after filing to submit your full schedules, means test, and other required documents.

What happens if I do not complete the filing within 14 days?

If you fail to file the remaining schedules and documents within 14 days, the court will dismiss your case. This means the automatic stay goes away and creditors can resume collection actions. You may also face restrictions on refiling.

Can I stop a foreclosure sale with an emergency filing?

Yes. The automatic stay under 11 U.S.C. section 362 halts all foreclosure proceedings the instant the petition is filed. New York uses judicial foreclosure with a mandatory settlement conference, which takes 12-18 months, so you often have time to plan. But if a sale date is imminent, an emergency filing can stop it.
