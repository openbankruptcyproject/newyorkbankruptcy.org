Chapter 7 Eligibility in New York

Chapter 7 eliminates most unsecured debt in 3-4 months, but you must qualify. The primary gatekeeper is the means test. Here is what New York filers need to know.

This page provides general educational information, not legal advice. Consult a qualified attorney for advice about your specific situation.

New York Means Test Income Limits

The means test compares your average monthly income over the last 6 calendar months to New York's median income. If you are below the median, you automatically qualify.

1 person$66,973$5,581
2 persons$82,282$6,857
3 persons$97,878$8,157
4 persons$119,164$9,930

Add $10,300 for each person beyond 4. Figures updated periodically by the U.S. Trustee Program.

Above-Median Income? You May Still Qualify

If your income exceeds the median, the means test subtracts allowed expenses (housing, transportation, healthcare, taxes, childcare, etc.) from your income. If the remaining disposable income is too low to fund a meaningful Chapter 13 plan, you still qualify for Chapter 7.

Key deductions include:

  • Actual mortgage or rent payments
  • IRS Local Standards for transportation and housing
  • Health insurance and out-of-pocket medical costs
  • Childcare and education expenses (up to $2,025/child/year)
  • Payroll taxes and mandatory retirement contributions
  • Secured debt payments (car loans, mortgage)
  • Priority debts (tax debts, domestic support obligations)

New York's high cost of living often means above-median filers still pass after deductions.

Who Cannot File Chapter 7

  • Fail the means test with no special circumstances -- you would need to file Chapter 13 instead
  • Prior Chapter 7 discharge within the last 8 years (measured from filing date to filing date) -- 11 U.S.C. section 727(a)(8)
  • Prior Chapter 13 discharge within the last 6 years (unless you paid 100% of unsecured claims or 70% in a good-faith plan) -- section 727(a)(9)
  • Prior dismissal for abuse within 180 days -- section 109(g)
  • No credit counseling -- you must complete an approved credit counseling course within 180 days before filing
  • Stockbrokers and commodity brokers -- must use SIPA liquidation instead

New York Exemption Choice

New York lets you choose between state and federal exemptions. Key state exemptions:

  • Homestead: $179,975--$215,550 depending on county
  • Vehicle: $4,825
  • Personal property: Various limits for clothing, furniture, tools of trade

The federal exemption set has a homestead of $27,900 (individual) but offers a more generous wildcard exemption ($1,475 + up to $13,950 of unused homestead). If you do not own a home, federal exemptions may protect more of your other property.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the income limit for Chapter 7 in New York?

The means test uses New York's median income: $66,973 for 1 person, $82,282 for 2, $97,878 for 3, $119,164 for 4. Below the median means automatic qualification. Above-median filers may still qualify after allowed expense deductions.

Can I choose between state and federal exemptions?

Yes. New York allows debtors to choose. State homestead: $179,975--$215,550 by county. Federal homestead: $27,900. The federal wildcard may be better if you do not own a home.

How often can I file Chapter 7?

You must wait 8 years between Chapter 7 filing dates. If you received a Chapter 13 discharge, you must wait 6 years unless you paid 100% of unsecured claims or 70% in a good-faith plan. Check with the 1328(f) screener.

Where do I file in New York?

The S.D.N.Y. has courthouses at One Bowling Green in Manhattan and 300 Quarropas Street in White Plains. Filing is typically done electronically through an attorney's CM/ECF account.
