Bankruptcy Timeline in New York

A step-by-step timeline for both Chapter 7 (3-4 months) and Chapter 13 (3-5 years) bankruptcy in the Southern District of New York.

Chapter 7 Timeline

1-4 Weeks Before Filing

Preparation

Gather required documents: 6 months of pay stubs, 2 years of tax returns, bank statements, list of all debts and assets. Complete the required pre-filing credit counseling course (must be taken within 180 days before filing, costs $15-25). Consult with an attorney. Review filing checklist.

Day 1 -- Filing Day

Petition Filed

Your attorney files the petition, schedules, and statements with the U.S. Bankruptcy Court. The automatic stay takes effect immediately -- all collection activity, garnishments, foreclosures, and repossessions must stop. Filing fee: $338.

Day 21-40

341 Meeting of Creditors

You attend a brief meeting of creditors (usually by phone or video for S.D.N.Y. cases). The Chapter 7 trustee asks questions under oath about your finances. Most meetings last 5-10 minutes. Bring photo ID and proof of Social Security number.

Day 30-60

Debtor Education Course

Complete the second required financial management course ($10-25). This must be done before discharge can be entered. File the certificate with the court.

Day 60-75

Deadline for Objections

Creditors have 60 days from the 341 meeting to file objections to discharge or to specific debts. In most cases, no objections are filed.

Day 90-120

Discharge Entered

The court enters your discharge order, permanently eliminating eligible debts. The case closes shortly after. The discharge injunction prevents creditors from ever collecting on discharged debts.

Chapter 13 Timeline

1-4 Weeks Before Filing

Preparation

Same document gathering as Chapter 7, plus income projections and a proposed repayment plan. Complete credit counseling course. Your attorney drafts the Chapter 13 plan. Review filing checklist.

Day 1

Petition and Plan Filed

Petition filed with the court. The automatic stay takes effect immediately. The proposed repayment plan is filed (or within 14 days). Filing fee: $313.

Day 1-30

First Plan Payment

You must begin making plan payments to the Chapter 13 trustee within 30 days of filing, even before the plan is confirmed. Payments are typically made via payroll deduction.

Day 21-50

341 Meeting of Creditors

Similar to Chapter 7 but the Chapter 13 trustee reviews your proposed plan and budget. Creditors may ask questions or raise objections to the plan.

Day 45-90

Plan Confirmation Hearing

The court holds a hearing to confirm (approve) your repayment plan. Creditor objections are resolved. Once confirmed, the plan is binding on all parties.

Year 1-5

Plan Payments

You make monthly payments to the Chapter 13 trustee, who distributes funds to creditors. Below-median-income debtors may complete a 3-year plan. Above-median debtors typically must complete a 5-year plan.

After Final Payment

Discharge

After completing all plan payments and the debtor education course, the court enters your Chapter 13 discharge. Remaining unsecured debt balances are eliminated.

S.D.N.Y. Courthouses

  • Manhattan: One Bowling Green, New York, NY 10004
  • White Plains: 300 Quarropas St, White Plains, NY 10601

Your case assignment depends on the county where you reside. Manhattan and boroughs cases are heard at Bowling Green. Westchester and suburban county cases are heard in White Plains. Look up your district.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does Chapter 7 take in New York?

Typically 3-4 months from filing to discharge. The 341 meeting happens around day 30, and discharge is entered about 60-90 days after that.

How long does Chapter 13 take?

Chapter 13 involves a 3-5 year repayment plan. Below-median-income debtors may qualify for 3 years; above-median typically must complete 5 years. Discharge is entered after the final plan payment.

What happens at the 341 meeting?

A brief hearing (5-10 minutes) where the trustee asks questions under oath about your finances. Creditors rarely attend. Bring photo ID and proof of SSN. Full guide.

How long does it take to prepare for filing?

Usually 1-4 weeks to gather documents and complete credit counseling. Emergency filings can be done in a few days if facing imminent garnishment, repossession, or foreclosure. See filing checklist.
