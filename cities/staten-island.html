Bankruptcy in Staten Island, NY

Staten Island is the southernmost and least densely populated borough of New York City. Richmond County has a more suburban character than the other boroughs, with higher homeownership rates and a population of approximately 475,000.

New York Exemptions (State or Federal) in Staten Island

New York allows debtors to choose between state and federal bankruptcy exemptions. New York allows a choice between state and federal exemptions. State homestead: $179,975 in Richmond County. Federal homestead: $27,900. State exemptions include: vehicle $4,550, cash $5,000, household goods up to $14,375, tools of trade $11,975, and jewelry $1,150. Federal exemptions offer: $27,900 homestead, $4,450 vehicle, and a wildcard of $1,475 plus up to $13,950 unused homestead. Retirement accounts are fully exempt under both systems.

Exemptions determine what property you can keep in bankruptcy. See the full exemptions guide.

Filing Bankruptcy in Staten Island, NY

The Process

  1. Credit counseling - Complete an approved course within 180 days before filing ($15-25).
  2. File your petition - Filed with the Eastern District of New York. The automatic stay takes effect immediately.
  3. 341 meeting - About 30 days after filing, attend a brief meeting of creditors at or near 271 Cadman Plaza East, Brooklyn, NY 11201.
  4. Debtor education - Complete a second financial course before discharge ($10-25).
  5. Discharge - Chapter 7: approximately 60 days after the 341 meeting. Chapter 13: after completing 3-5 year plan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Where do Staten Island residents file bankruptcy?

Staten Island residents file in the Eastern District of New York at 271 Cadman Plaza East, Brooklyn, NY 11201. While less convenient than a local courthouse, it is accessible via the Staten Island Ferry and subway.

Can I protect my Staten Island home in bankruptcy?

New York's state homestead exemption for Richmond County is $179,975. Staten Island has higher homeownership rates and home values than other boroughs. If your equity exceeds the exemption, Chapter 13 allows you to keep your home while repaying debts over 3-5 years.

Is Staten Island in a different district than Brooklyn?

No. Staten Island (Richmond County) and Brooklyn (Kings County) are both in the Eastern District of New York. The Bronx and Manhattan are in the Southern District. This matters because different districts may have different local rules and trustee practices.
