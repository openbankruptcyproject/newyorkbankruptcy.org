Small Business Bankruptcy in New York

New York small businesses have several bankruptcy options depending on whether the goal is to close the business or reorganize and continue operating. Subchapter V has made reorganization faster and more affordable.

Subchapter V - Small Business Reorganization

Subchapter V of Chapter 11, created by the Small Business Reorganization Act of 2019, is specifically designed for small businesses:

  • Debt limit: Total noncontingent, liquidated debts under $7.5 million (at least 50% from business activity)
  • No creditors committee: Reduces cost and complexity
  • No disclosure statement: The plan goes directly to the court for confirmation
  • Debtor keeps ownership: No requirement for creditor consent to confirm the plan (cramdown available without the absolute priority rule)
  • Dedicated Sub V trustee: Facilitates the process (but does not take control of the business)
  • 90-day plan deadline: The plan must be filed within 90 days of the petition date
  • 3-5 year plan: Disposable income is paid to creditors over 3-5 years

Chapter 7 Business Liquidation

If the business cannot be saved, Chapter 7 liquidation provides an orderly wind-down:

  • A trustee takes control of business assets and sells them to pay creditors
  • The business ceases operations
  • For sole proprietors, personal liability for business debts can be discharged
  • LLCs and corporations do not receive a discharge - they simply cease to exist
  • Personal guarantees on business debts may need to be addressed in a separate personal filing

Sole Proprietors: Personal and Business Debts

If you operate as a sole proprietor (no LLC or corporate structure), your personal and business debts are intertwined. Options include:

  • Chapter 7: Eliminates personal liability for business debts. The business closes, but you get a fresh start.
  • Chapter 13: Keep operating your business while repaying debts over 3-5 years. Good for sole proprietors who want to continue the business.
  • Subchapter V: Available to individuals with business debts comprising at least 50% of total debt.

New York exemptions protect tools of trade up to $3,000, which can include business equipment for sole proprietors.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the bankruptcy options for small businesses in New York?

Chapter 7 (liquidation to close), Chapter 11 (reorganization), Subchapter V (streamlined reorganization for debts under $7.5M), and Chapter 13 for sole proprietors. The best option depends on whether you want to continue operating.

What is Subchapter V bankruptcy?

A streamlined Chapter 11 for businesses with under $7.5M in debt. No creditors committee, no disclosure statement, the debtor keeps ownership, and the plan must be filed within 90 days. It is faster and cheaper than traditional Chapter 11.

Can I keep my business open during bankruptcy?

Yes, in Chapter 11 and Subchapter V. The business operates as a debtor-in-possession while reorganizing. In Chapter 7, the business typically closes. The automatic stay protects the business during the case.

How much does small business bankruptcy cost?

Filing fees: Chapter 7 ($338), Chapter 11 ($1,738), Subchapter V ($571). Attorney fees for Chapter 11 in New York typically range from $15,000-$50,000+. Subchapter V cases generally cost less. Cost breakdown.
