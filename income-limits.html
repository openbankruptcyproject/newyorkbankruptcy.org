Bankruptcy Income Limits in New York

Income determines which chapter you qualify for and how much you pay. Here are the current thresholds for New York residents filing in the S.D.N.Y.

What Counts as Income

The means test uses your average monthly income from the 6 full calendar months before filing. It includes:

  • Wages, salary, tips, overtime, bonuses, commissions
  • Net self-employment or business income
  • Rental and investment income
  • Pension and retirement distributions
  • Unemployment compensation and workers' compensation
  • Child support and alimony received
  • Regular contributions from a non-filing spouse or household member

What Does NOT Count

  • Social Security benefits (for the means test comparison, though they count for disposable income in Chapter 13)
  • Payments to victims of war crimes or terrorism

Timing strategy: Because the test uses a 6-month lookback, filing during a period of lower income (after job loss, end of seasonal work, after a bonus period ends) can help you qualify for Chapter 7.

Above Median? You May Still Qualify

Being above the median does not automatically disqualify you from Chapter 7. The second part of the means test subtracts allowed expenses from your income. New York's high cost of living means many above-median filers still pass after deducting:

  • Actual mortgage or rent payments
  • Property taxes and insurance
  • Health insurance and medical costs
  • Childcare and education
  • Payroll taxes
  • Court-ordered payments
  • Vehicle loan and lease payments

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the income limit for Chapter 7?

New York's median for 1 person is $66,973. Below = auto-qualify. Above-median filers may still qualify after expense deductions. See Chapter 7 eligibility for details.

Is there an income limit for Chapter 13?

No ceiling. Income determines your plan length (3 or 5 years) and payment amount, not eligibility. The debt limit is $2,750,000. See Chapter 13 eligibility.

Can I make too much to file any bankruptcy?

No. If you earn too much for Chapter 7, file Chapter 13. If debts exceed Chapter 13 limits, file Chapter 11. There is always an option.

Does my spouse's income count?

If you are married and not filing jointly, your spouse's income is included on the means test form but is then subtracted if the spouse is not paying for household expenses. A non-filing spouse's income that contributes to household expenses is counted.
